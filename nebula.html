NEBULA

A nebula is a breathtaking celestial wonder, often referred to as the nurseries of stars." These vast clouds of gas and dust are scattered across galaxies, forming some of the most spectacular sights in the universe. Nebulae play a crucial role in the life cycle of stars, acting as both their birthplaces and remnants after they die. They are composed primarily of hydrogen and helium, with traces of other elements that give them their vibrant colors, often captured in stunning images by telescopes like Hubble. There are several types of nebulae, including emission nebulae, which glow brightly as their gases are energized by nearby stars, and reflection nebulae, which shine by reflecting starlight.
